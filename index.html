Sous Vide

What is Sous Vide?

Sous vide, a French term meaning "under vacuum," is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in airtight bags and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled, low temperature. This method ensures even cooking throughout, preserving the food's natural juices, flavours, and nutrients while achieving unmatched tenderness and texture. Popular among chefs and home cooks alike, sous vide is ideal for cooking meats, fish, vegetables, and even desserts with consistent and professional-quality results. The process often requires finishing touches, such as searing, to develop a crispy exterior for a visually and texturally appealing dish.

What do you need?

Sous Vide Machine

The machine that will regulate the temperature to the accuracy of 0.1°C for more than a few hours.

Pot

A pot that you can keep the water in for the whole process, best if insulated.

Vacuum Sealer (Optional)

A vacuum sealer that can seal food airtight in bags, preserving its freshness and enabling even cooking in sous vide.

Frying Pan (Optional)

A pan ideal for searing, sautéing, and frying, often used to add a crispy finish to sous vide dishes.

Kitchen Torch (Optional)

A handheld tool for caramelising, browning, or charring food surfaces, adding flavour and visual appeal.

BBQ Grill (Optional)

A cooking device that uses direct heat for grilling or smoking, perfect for achieving that signature smoky taste.
